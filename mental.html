Mental Health

Mental health talks about our emotional, social, and psychological well-being. It is simply the same as emotional health as it also involves the way we feel and act with coping up with life. Mental health is both important from childhood to adolescence and to being an adult. Mental health is also related to decision making as it involes the mind of every person.

Mental Disorders

Mental disorders are disorders that involves the way we think and act. Having mental disorders can greatly affect your mental well-being as it affects the way we act towards others and the way we think. Mental disorders may or may not be cured easily. When you suffer from this kind of disorder, you'll have a hard time trying to interact with the persons around you and you will not be living from your usual routine anymore.

Having mental disorders are common, so don't worry about you being different from others. Mental disorders can be treated. Most of the people that has mental disorders that went through the process of those treatments have all been better and they also completely recovered from it.

Factors that can Affect your Mental Health

If you want to know how your mental health is affected, here are some factors you should know:

1. Family history
If you have a mental disorder, even if you feel like you are fine, you'll still have a mental disorder if your family has a history of mental health problems.
2. Lifestyle
If you have an unhealthy lifestyle, it is possible that you'll soon experience mental health problems. This is caused by the things you take in and your unhealthy diet.
3. Traumatic experiences.
This is the most common causes of a person having mental health problems. Having traumatic experiences in life can greatly affect your mind as these experiences will never be forgotten in the mind of a person. It is forever placed in the mind of the person.

Importance of Mental Health

Mental health is a very important aspect of our health as it involves the way we think. To keep our mental health safe and healthy, we should take care of it. As we all know, having a good mental health is great for our bodies. Having a good mental health, also means good physical health. Mental health is important because:

  • It can keep you physically healthy.
  • It can produce healthy relationships.
  • It can help you realize your true potential or your skills.
  • It keeps you from working productively.
  • It can guide you in making remarkable contributions in your community.

Signs that you might be experiencing a Mental Health Problem

Sometimes when determining our own emotions or the emotions of others, it may be hard to determine what is normal or not. So when it comes to mental health problems, you may not know whether you have problems in your mental health. Because of this a lot of people are not aware. So, these are some warning signs that can determine whether you are mentally sick:

1. Experiencing unexplainble aches and pains.
When you suddenly experience an unexplainable ache or pain out of nowhere, it is a warning or your body's like warning you that it wants rest. This usually happens to people who usually works all time or all day and night.
2. Feeling like nothing matters to you anymore.
It simply means that you feel numb. You feel like everything doesn't matter anymore. You feel like your mind is very light as it doesn't have any problems or thoughts to think anymore.
3. You avoid doing physical activities and people.
You avoid the activities that you usually enjoyed and you tend to avoid people, even your family members. You tend to overthink at this stage and you usually feel anxious.
4. Regularly experiencing mood swings.
Your emotions rapidly changes regularly causing you to lose some relationships. Usual mood swings causes people to avoid you, because they think that you're weird and you don't act like a normal person.
5. Hurting yourself and others.
Having mental health problems, causes you to think very crazy ideas, like the thought of killing a person. Because of the way you think, it can affect your behaviors which can sometimes lead to hurting yourself and others.
6. Changed eating or sleeping habits.
You tend to forget to eat for a whole day or not take a sleep for a whole night, or even a nap.
7. Having no energy to do anything.
You tend to feel more tired causing you to be more lazy. Being lazy can affect your body physique and your physical health.

If you have experienced some of these warning signs or maybe most of them, it means you have a mental health problem. But you also need to consult a professional just to be sure. If you really do have, try taking therapy sessions and always consult a professional to help you through the healing process.
