Emotional Health

Emotional Health is the ability of a person to be able to cope up with both of its positive and negative emotions. It is one of the aspect of our mental health. In emotional health, you can also include your awareness on your emotions.

Being emotionally healthy means, that you have a good way or strategy of coping with your negative emotions and being emotionally healthy also means that you know when and where to ask for professional help.

Emotional wellness is really tied to physical health. It means how well you manage stress and negative emotions in your life.

A person who experiences great amounts of stress and negative emotions will likely develop health problems, such as cancer and heart-related diseases. These health problems are not caused by the negative emotions felt by the person, but the behavoir of a person who experiences such negative emotions. A good example for this are smoking cigarettes or tobaccos and drinking alcohol. This actions or behaviors are done by this kind of people to help reduce the negative emotions they feel such as stress. But instead of helping them, they just make the condition of the person more worse. They do reduce the negative emotions felt, however doing these actions can likely make the person sick or if continuous doing of these actions, it may lead to the person's death.

Always remember that, emotional health is more than emotion regulation and coping skills. These strategies done to lower the negative emotions, can be used by every person whether you have a mental illness or not.

Signs that you are having a hard time in Taking care of your Emotional Health

If you want to know whether you are having a hard time in maintaing your emotional health, here are some warning signs that you should know:

  • You regularly sleep too much or too little.
  • Your performance is lower than usual.
  • When you isolate yourself from others or from the outside world.
  • You feel and look more tired.
  • Avoids personal care and hygiene.
  • You feel irritated, guilty, and worthless.
  • You always fill your mind with a lot of thoughts.
  • You eat too much or too little.

Ways to take care of your Emotional Health

If you experience any of the warning signs mentioned above, here are some ways that you can do to improve them:

1. Make time to Meditate.
Meditating is a very effictive approach in improving your emotional health. Meditating can calm down, not only your thoughts, but also your whole body. Meditating helps you in focusing on how to breathe in and out air properly and in clearing your thoughts.
2. Make a Sleep Schedule.
Making a sleep schedule for yourself, allows you to have a proper sleep. You need to have the usual 7-8 hours of sleep. Having a 7-8 hours of sleep can help your body perform properly
when you wake up in the morning. Sleeping late is not really that bad, but if you don't usually sleep for 7-8 hours, you are more likely to feel tired.
3. Balance your Lifestyle.
Having a proper work and a proper life is very great to have. But you have to always balance both of your work and your personal life. It doesn't mean that if you have a
very strict kind of work that you need to forget about your personal life. Always remind yourself that you are a person too, a human being, just like everybody else.
4. Connect with the Real World.
You need to socialize with other people to better manage your emotions. Get off of your phone, go outside and have fun with nature.
Have time to bond with your family, friends, and relatives. Meeting people virtually is nice, but personally making friends is much better.
5. Do Exercises.
Doing exercises does not only keep you physically fit and healthy, but can also improve your emotional. These exercices can be intense or not, it depends on what you can do to just stretch your body.
6. Appreciate Yourself.
Talking bad about yourself is very bad for your emotional health. Doing this can give you a negative mindset about yourself and it can give you insecurities and anxiety.
If you want to look good, start from your words, your mind will soon follow.
7. Set goals and appreciate your achievements.
Set goals for the things you want to achieve in the future. Always appreciate yourself when you make a new achievement. You can treat yourself to something nice to celebrate for it.
8. Always keep in mind when and where to seek help.
This is the most important way to improve your emotional health. Knowing where and when to seek help would be much easier than not being prepared.
